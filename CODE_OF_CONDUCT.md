# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

Lodgr is an open source project and we want it to be a welcoming place for everyone who wants to contribute. We expect everyone involved in the project to treat others with respect, regardless of experience level, background, or opinion.

Behaviour that is welcome:

- Being helpful and patient with people who are new to the project or to open source
- Giving constructive feedback on code without being dismissive or unkind
- Disagreeing professionally and focusing on the technical merits of a discussion
- Acknowledging when you are wrong and learning from it

Behaviour that is not acceptable:

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory comments of any kind
- Dismissing or belittling contributions from less experienced developers
- Deliberately derailing discussions or being intentionally disruptive
- Publishing someone's private information without their consent

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication related to the Lodgr project.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this code of conduct, please report it by opening a private discussion or contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be handled confidentially.

Maintainers reserve the right to remove comments, close issues, or ban contributors who repeatedly or seriously violate these standards.

---

This is a plain-language code of conduct. We are not trying to cover every possible scenario with rules, just asking people to be reasonable and decent.
